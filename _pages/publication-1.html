Publications
Performance of enhanced geothermal system with varying injection-production parameters and reservoir properties
KaixuanWang, ZaobaoLiu, TaoZeng, FeiWang, WanqingShen, JianfuShao
A coupled thermo-hydro-mechanical (THM) simulation of 3D fractured geothermal reservoirs reveals that increasing fracture density enhances heat extraction until a plateau threshold, while sensitivity analysis quantitatively identifies fracture and matrix permeability as dominant factors exhibiting nonlinear impacts on performance.

Micromechanical modeling of work hardening for coupling microstructure evolution, dynamic recovery and recrystallization: Application to high entropy alloys
X He, L Liu, T Zeng, Y Yao
A dislocation density-based micromechanical model is developed for high-entropy alloys, capturing all hardening stages, incorporating a new recrystallization model, proposing a novel macroscopic inelastic strain determination method, and accounting for stiffness differences between cell walls and interiors.

Crystal plasticity model to predict fatigue crack nucleation based on the phase transformation theory
L Liu, J Wang, T Zeng, Y Yao
A crystal plasticity model predicts fatigue crack nucleation in polycrystals by modeling accumulated dislocation dipoles as damage origin, using a slip system-level damage law within FE analysis to capture cyclic softening and predict critical cycles via a modified phase transformation model, validated experimentally.

A micromechanical model for the elastic–plastic behavior of porous rocks
T Zeng, JF Shao, WY Xu
A polycrystalline model for sandstone's elastic-plastic behavior represents its microstructure as discrete grains and pores, modeling grain-scale plasticity through pressure-dependent frictional sliding on weakness planes with explicit volumetric dilatancy. The framework employs self-consistent homogenization to resolve grain-pore interactions, implements an efficient numerical procedure, and systematically validates predictions against experimental data to capture key mechanical features.

A micromechanical analysis to the elasto-viscoplastic behavior of solder alloys
L Liu, Y Yao, T Zeng
A small-strain multiscale elasto-plastic self-consistent model is developed for solder alloys (Sn-3.0Ag-0.5Cu/Sn-0.7Cu), capturing rate- and temperature-dependent responses via a modified Voce hardening law. Integrated into FE analysis with micro-macro stress updating algorithms, it quantifies grain shape effects and is validated through Taylor factor analysis and experimental data across temperatures/strain rates, demonstrating accurate predictions.

Multiscale modeling of cohesive geomaterials with a polycrystalline approach
T Zeng, JF Shao, WY Xu
A polycrystalline model for cohesive geomaterials (e.g., granite) captures macroscopic elastic-plastic behavior through pressure-sensitive crystallographic slip systems and non-associated plastic potentials for dilatancy. It modifies stress concentration laws to link macro-micro stresses, addresses computational implementation challenges, and validates predictions via conventional and true triaxial compression tests.

An upscaled model for elastoplastic behavior of the Callovo-Oxfordian argillite
T Zeng, JF Shao, Y Yao
This paper proposes a micromechanical model for the elastoplastic behavior of Callovo-Oxfordian (COx) argillite, using a macro-meso-micro approach. At the mesoscale, COx is a three-phase composite (porous clay matrix, calcite, quartz). The porous clay uses an extended GTN model; minerals are elastic. Homogenization employs Hill’s incremental formulation. Validation involves unit cell FE simulations and experimental data comparisons.

A micromechanical model considering dislocation density based intra-granular backstress under cyclic loading
L Liu, Y Yao, T Zeng, LM Keer
Cyclic experiments on Sn-3.0Ag-0.5Cu solder across temperatures/strain rates inform a dislocation density-based micromechanical model for polycrystals, incorporating reversible dislocations for path-change effects and an Eshelby-derived slip-level backstress to capture Bauschinger behavior; validated via self-consistent modeling on SAC305 and modified 9Cr-1Mo steel.

A dislocation density based micromechanical constitutive model for Sn–Ag–Cu solder alloys
L Liu, Y Yao, T Zeng, LM Keer
A micromechanical model for Sn-Ag-Cu solders incorporates precipitate effects via dislocation density hardening, modeling IMCs as particles in β-Sn matrix. Using EPSC and Mori-Tanaka schemes, it captures dislocation blocking/stiffness enhancement, predicts thermo-viscoplastic behavior and IMC-modulated Young's modulus, validated experimentally.

Modeling of viscoplastic deformation in geomaterials with a polycrystalline approach
T Zeng, JF Shao, WY Xu
A micromechanical model for cohesive frictional geomaterials attributes macroscopic viscoplasticity to subcritical sliding on weakness planes, capturing pressure sensitivity and dilatancy via local yield criteria and non-associated potentials. It employs a simplified interaction law for scale transition, details computational implementation, and is validated against granite triaxial/creep tests.
